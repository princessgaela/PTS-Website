Lingayen Capitol

SPOTS REVIEW:

The beautiful Capitol of Lingayen in Pangasinan, built in 1918 during the American colonial period, is an outstanding piece of neoclassic state architecture that has survived the ravages of World War II.
The buildings surrounding the area are for government offices. However, the main capitol building is open for tourists. Inside, the flooring, furniture, handrails and even the stairs are made of polished wood that screams how classy and traditional the building is at the same time.

WHAT TO KNOW?

LOCATION:

Lingayen,Pangasinan.

FEE(S):

none

BEST FOR:

->Travel enthusiasm,Architectural Treasures.

HIGHLIGHT(S):

->Capitol Building’s neoclassical architecture has been preserved, and over the years, its beauty and prestige have been well-maintained and restored.

INSIDER INFO:

->The Capitol building was used as the screen for the 3D projection presentation of Pangasinan’s history and development with complements of lights and sounds and a fireworks display at the end of the presentation.
